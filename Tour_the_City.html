Start Your City Tour from Lakeview

Staying in Lakeview? It's a great neighborhood to launch your tour of the city. Here's a suggested itinerary for seeing a lot of sights in one day, and is meant for warm weather days.


Grab a Divvy Bike

Divvy is Chicago's bikesharing program. It allows you to pick up a bike at one of many locations throughout the city and drop them off at another location. It's a great way to cover a lot of ground but still have time to take in your surroundings.

Grab a bike from a station and head to the lakefront path!

Divvy Bike locations and rates can be found on their website.


The Lakefront Path

Ride the scenic lakefront path south towards the city. As you ride, you'll see Lincoln Park on your right and Lake Michigan on your left. There are a number of spots along the way that make for great photo opportunities of the Chicago skyline.


Oak Street Beach

There are a few places that make for good stopping points along the lakefront path, but Oak Street Beach is our favorite. In addition to the beach itself, there is a large outdoor bar that's open in the summer and is great for relaxing with a view of the lake in front of you and the skyline behind you.


The Chicago River

Continue your ride south and cross the bridge that spans the Chicago river. While on the brigdge, you can see the locks that allow boats to enter the river from Lake Michigan to the east, as well as Navy Pier and its large Ferris Wheel. To your right, you have a great view of the Chicago River and the downtown buildings that run along it.


Maggie Daley and Millenium Parks

Continue your ride south, which will wind to the right towards large, open park land. Drop off your bike at the nearest Divvy station and take a walk through Maggie Daley Park, with its playgrounds and hills, over the winding pedestrian bridge and into Millenium Park. Millenium Park has the large, open air Pritzker Pavilion, beautiful Lurie Garden, rotating public art and home to the Bean, Chicago's most photographed attraction. The Art Institute is also nearby if you want to take a detour!


Cindy's at the Chicago Athletic Association

12 S Michigan Ave, Chicago, IL

It's probably time to take a break. Head over to Cindy's, the rooftop bar of the Chicago Athletic Association hotel. Grab a bite to eat and drink on their terrace with a view of Millenium Park, Maggie Daley Park and Lake Michigan.


The Water Taxi

Walk a few blocks north across the Chicago River to the Wrigley Building. From here, you can pick up the Water Taxi towards Chinatown. This is another great way to take a break while you get an upclose view of the Chicago's famous riverfront architecture.

Water Taxi rates, pick up locations and more information can be found on their website.


Ping Tom Memorial Park

The Water Taxi will drop you off in Chinatown on the banks of Ping Tom Memorial Park, named after Chinatown's most noted civic leaders. It's a short walk to the restaurants on Chinatown Square and Wentworth Avenue.


Dinner in Chinatown

Chinatown has numerous restaurants to choose from, and for the most part you can't go wrong. Our favorites are Phoenix and Lao Sze Schuan.

The El back to Lakeview

Chinatown is an easy ride on the CTA Red line, which will take back north to Lakeview.
